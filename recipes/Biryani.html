Description

Introducing our treasured family recipe for authentic Mutton Dum Biryani!

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Whole Spices
  • Mutton
  • Ground Spices
  • Herbs
  • Ghee

Steps

  1. Make the marinade by mixing yogurt, ginger, garlic, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, salt, mint leaves, and lemon juice.
  2. Add Mutton and coat evenly with the marinade. Keep in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. You can marinate the Mutton overnight too.
  3. While the Mutton is marinating, rinse and drain the rice 2 to 3 times, then soak in water for 20 minutes.
  4. While the rice is soaking, add ghee to a heavy-bottomed pan and add onions.
  5. On medium heat, fry the onions stirring frequently until they start to turn golden brown - about 15 to 20 minutes.
  6. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the boiling water. Bring the rice to a full boil on high heat. Lower the heat to medium and cook uncovered for 5 to 6 minutes or until the rice is about 90% cooked. Drain the rice promptly and reserve.
  7. Cover the pot and seal it with aluminum foil, then place some weight on top of the lid. Cook on low heat for 20 minutes, this is the steam or "Dum" cooking step. Turn the heat off and wait another 10 minutes before opening the pot.
